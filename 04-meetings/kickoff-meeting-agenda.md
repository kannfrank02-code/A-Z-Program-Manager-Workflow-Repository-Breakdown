# Kickoff Meeting Agenda

Use this agenda for the first official meeting of a program, project, initiative, workflow change, event, training rollout, or operational improvement effort.

The goal of the kickoff meeting is to make sure everyone understands the purpose, scope, timeline, roles, risks, communication plan, and next steps.

## Meeting Purpose

The purpose of this meeting is to officially launch the program and align all key stakeholders before work begins.

By the end of this meeting, the team should understand:

- Why the program exists
- What the program is expected to accomplish
- What is included
- What is not included
- Who owns each part of the work
- What the timeline looks like
- What risks or dependencies may affect the work
- How communication and reporting will happen
- What needs to happen next

## Agenda

### 1. Welcome and Introductions

Purpose:

Make sure everyone knows who is in the room and why they are involved.

Discussion Prompts:

- Please share your name, role, and connection to this program.
- What department or team are you representing?
- What do you need from this program to be successful?

### 2. Program Background

Purpose:

Explain how the program started and why it matters.

Discussion Prompts:

- What business need created this program?
- What problem are we solving?
- What opportunity are we trying to capture?
- What happens if this program is delayed or not completed?

### 3. Program Goals

Purpose:

Confirm what success looks like.

Discussion Prompts:

- What are the top goals of this program?
- What outcomes are expected?
- How will we measure success?
- What does leadership care most about?

Example Goals:

- Improve workflow efficiency
- Reduce manual follow-up
- Improve reporting visibility
- Increase stakeholder alignment
- Improve user adoption
- Complete implementation by deadline

### 4. Scope Review

Purpose:

Confirm what is included in the program.

Discuss:

- Work included
- Deliverables included
- Teams included
- Systems or tools included
- Required documentation
- Required training
- Required approvals

Put final scope details in the Program Charter.

### 5. Out-of-Scope Review

Purpose:

Prevent scope creep.

Discuss:

- What is not included?
- What requests should be handled separately?
- What decisions are outside this program?
- What work requires separate approval?

Example:

The program may include training coordination, but not software development.  
The program may include process documentation, but not hiring decisions.

### 6. Timeline and Milestones

Purpose:

Review the expected timeline.

Discuss:

- Start date
- Planning deadline
- Major milestones
- Testing or review periods
- Training dates
- Launch date
- Closeout date

### 7. Roles and Responsibilities

Purpose:

Make sure every major task has an owner.

Discuss:

- Who owns the program?
- Who approves decisions?
- Who owns each workstream?
- Who provides subject matter expertise?
- Who manages communications?
- Who manages training?
- Who manages budget?
- Who escalates risks?

Role Table:

|---|---|---|

| Business Owner |  | Owns business outcome |

| Workstream Lead |  | Owns assigned workstream deliverables |
| Finance Partner |  | Supports budget, cost center, invoices, and expense tracking |
| Communications Lead |  | Supports messaging and stakeholder updates |
| Training Lead |  | Supports training and adoption |
| Vendor Partner |  | Supports external deliverables |

### 8. Risks and Dependencies

Purpose:

Identify anything that could delay or disrupt the program.

Discuss:

- What could go wrong?
- What approvals are needed?
- What teams are dependent on each other?
- What resources are limited?
- What deadlines are tight?
- What decisions are pending?

|---|---|---|---|
|  |  |  |  |

### 9. Communication Plan

Purpose:

Confirm how updates will be shared.

Discuss:

- Who needs weekly updates?
- Who only needs milestone updates?
- What should be escalated?
- What channel will be used?
- How often will status reports be sent?

Communication Table:

| Audience | Type of Update | Frequency | Channel | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Executive Sponsor | Executive summary | Weekly | Email | Program Manager |
| Workstream Leads | Status/action items | Weekly | Meeting/Email | Program Manager |
| Stakeholders | Program updates | As needed | Email/Teams | Program Manager |
| End Users | Training/launch updates | As needed | Email/Teams | Training Lead |

### 10. Reporting Cadence

Purpose:

Confirm how progress will be reported.

Discuss:

- Weekly status report
- Executive summary
- RAID log updates
- Budget updates
- Milestone dashboard
- Leadership escalations

Reporting expectations:

- Status reports will be updated weekly.
- Risks and issues will be reviewed weekly.
- Decisions needed will be escalated quickly.
- Budget concerns will be reported as soon as they are identified.

## Meeting Close

Before ending the meeting, confirm:

- Does everyone understand the program purpose?
- Does everyone understand their role?
- Are there any concerns that were not discussed?
- Are there any missing stakeholders?
- Are there any immediate risks?
- Are the next steps clear?

## After the Meeting

The Program Manager should complete the following within 24 hours:

- Send meeting recap
- Share meeting notes
- Update project plan
- Update RAID log
- Update stakeholder register
- Save all documents
- Confirm next meeting
- Follow up on open questions
