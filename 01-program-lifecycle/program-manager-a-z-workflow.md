# A–Z Program Manager Workflow

This document explains what a Program Manager does from the beginning of a program to the end.

## A — Assess the Need

Do this first.

Ask:
- What is the problem?
- Who requested this?
- Why is this important?
- What happens if nothing is done?
- Who is impacted?

Put the answers in the Program Intake Form.

## B — Build the Business Case

Explain why the program matters.

Document:
- Business need
- Expected value
- Risk of delay
- Cost impact
- Operational impact

Put this in the Program Charter.

## C — Confirm the Sponsor

Identify the person with authority.

Document:
- Executive sponsor
- Business owner
- Decision maker
- Escalation contact

Put this in the Program Charter and Stakeholder Register.

## D — Define the Scope

Clarify what is included and what is not included.

Document:
- In scope items
- Out of scope items
- Assumptions
- Constraints
- Dependencies

Put this in the Program Charter.

## E — Establish Governance

Set rules for how the program will be managed.

Document:
- Meeting cadence
- Reporting cadence
- Decision process
- Approval process
- Escalation path

Put this in the Governance Model.

## F — Form the Team

Identify the people doing the work.

Document:
- Program Manager
- Workstream leads
- Subject matter experts
- Vendors
- Support teams

Put this in the Stakeholder Register and Resource Plan.

## G — Generate the Plan

Build the actual program plan.

Document:
- Tasks
- Owners
- Due dates
- Milestones
- Dependencies
- Status

Put this in the Project Plan Template.

## H — Hold the Kickoff Meeting

Schedule the first official meeting.

Use:
- Kickoff Meeting Agenda
- Program Charter
- Stakeholder Register
- Project Plan

After the meeting, send a recap email.

## I — Identify Risks

List anything that could delay or damage the program.

Track:
- Risks
- Assumptions
- Issues
- Dependencies

Put this in the RAID Log.

## J — Join the Workstreams

Make sure teams are not working in silos.

Do this:
- Connect related teams
- Confirm handoffs
- Clarify dependencies
- Review shared deadlines

Put updates in the Project Plan.

## K — Keep Status Visible

Update the program tracker every week.

Update:
- Project Plan
- RAID Log
- Action Items
- Decision Log
- Weekly Status Report

## L — Lead the Meetings

Every meeting should have structure.

Use:
- Agenda
- Action item tracker
- Decision log
- Risk review
- Next steps

Put notes in the Meetings folder.

## M — Monitor Metrics

Track whether the program is successful.

Measure:
- Timeline
- Budget
- Adoption
- Quality
- Completion rate
- Stakeholder satisfaction

Put results in the Weekly Status Report.

## N — Navigate Change

When someone asks to change the program, do not just say yes.

Use the Change Request Template.

Document:
- What is changing
- Why it is changing
- Timeline impact
- Budget impact
- Resource impact
- Approval decision

## O — Organize Documentation

Keep all program files in one place.

Store:
- Charter
- Status reports
- Meeting notes
- RAID log
- Budget tracker
- Training materials
- Final closeout report

## P — Prepare Communications

Send clear updates.

Use:
- Status Update Email Template
- Escalation Email Template
- Executive Summary Template

## Q — Quality Check Deliverables

Before anything is marked complete, review it.

Check:
- Is it accurate?
- Is it complete?
- Was it approved?
- Does it meet the goal?
- Is documentation saved?

## R — Resolve Issues

For every issue, assign:
- Owner
- Due date
- Impact
- Resolution plan
- Status

Put it in the RAID Log.

## S — Support Adoption

Make sure people know how to use the new process, system, or service.

Use:
- Training Plan
- FAQ
- Office hours
- Job aids
- Feedback survey

## T — Track Budget and Resources

Monitor money and staffing.

Use:
- Budget Tracker
- Resource Plan
- Vendor Tracker

## U — Update Leadership

Send leadership the important information.

Include:
- Overall status
- Wins
- Risks
- Decisions needed
- Budget impact
- Timeline impact
- Next steps

Use the Executive Summary Template.

## V — Validate Outcomes

Compare the final results to the original goals.

Ask:
- Did we meet the goal?
- Was it completed on time?
- Was it completed within budget?
- Did stakeholders accept the result?

Put this in the Closeout Report.

## W — Wrap Up

Before closing the program, confirm:
- Deliverables are complete
- Files are saved
- Owners are assigned
- Open items are resolved
- Leadership has signed off

## X — Examine Lessons Learned

Document:
- What worked
- What failed
- What should change
- What should be repeated

Put this in the Closeout Report.

## Y — Yield Repeatable Processes

Turn successful work into templates.

Create:
- SOPs
- Checklists
- Trackers
- Communication templates
- Training materials

## Z — Zero In on Continuous Improvement

Update this repository after each program.

Improve:
- Templates
- Checklists
- Reporting tools
- Workflows
- Prompts
